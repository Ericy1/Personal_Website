NASA

Summer of 2021, I worked at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center to design and test software for a low cost optical ground terminal that will communicate with spacecraft from low earth to lunar orbit. Particularly, I am implementing a Gimbal Monitor & Control (GMC) system via Python, RabbitMQ, and Google Protobuf to enable communications between the ground-terminal telescope and a satellite acquisition/tracking software.

I also supported additional testing such as image data set processing with OpenCV and web scraping of satellite parameters for a satellite pass over Goddard Space Flight Center.

Jacobs Engineering

Summer of 2020, I automated flight telemetry analysis for the Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage and Multi-Purpose Crew Vehicle of the NASA Orion SLS (Artemis I) with Python to ensure nominal performance of tens of thousands of telemetry events. The framework was also designed to work on any future test run and development cycle. In the end, it resulted in >10x speedup of pre-delivery beta testing times.

I cannot wait to see the Orion SLS make its maiden flight in 2022!!!

Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

Summer of 2018, I worked with a LLNL postdoc to model the elemental composition and structure of exoplanets like COROT-7b through mass-radius relations. I also developed multiple Python scripts using the Preliminary Reference Earth Model as the baseline reference model to gather and analyze data.
