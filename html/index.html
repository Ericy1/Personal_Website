About Me

My name is Eric, and I'm a rising Junior studying Computer Science at Rice University (Graduating Fall 2022). I'm passionate about anything software related, particularly within the aerospace/defense industry. Last summer I worked under NASA's ESSCA contract with Jacobs to design automation testing for Orion SLS test runs. The Orion Space Launch System (SLS) is the most powerful rocket humanity has ever developed and will put humans back on the surface of the Moon. This summer I will be working with NASA to design and test software for an novel, optical communications terminal that will communicate with spacecraft from LEO to lunar orbit. I'll be using Python and HTTP sockets to calibrate/configure the ground terminal, Docker to support orchestration of software components, RabbitMQ for software interfacing, and a variety of frameworks for a user configurable UI for the ground station. In my free time I love to improve my photography skills, capturing mainly landscape, nature, and night-sky scenes (feel free to check out some of my photos!) I've also been speedcubing since 2012, which is the sport where you solve combination puzzles like the Rubik's Cube as fast as possible. I'm currently ranked top 400 in the world out of more than 140,000 competitors.
